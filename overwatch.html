Overview

Overwatch is a team-based multiplayer first-person shooter developed and published by Blizzard Entertainment. Described as a "hero shooter", Overwatch assigns players into two teams of six, with each player selecting from a roster of over 30 characters, known as "heroes", each with a unique style of play that is divided into three general roles that fit their purpose. Players on a team work together to secure and defend control points on a map or escort a payload across the map in a limited amount of time. Players gain cosmetic rewards that do not affect gameplay, such as character skins and victory poses, as they play the game. The game was initially launched with only casual play, but a competitive ranked mode, various 'arcade' game modes, and a player-customizable server browser were added after release.

Game Modes

  • Quick Play

    • Quick Play has two sub-modes. Quick Play - Classic is the classic game mode, where you are in an open queue for any game, and are able to choose any role and character. Quick Play - Role Queue is a game mode where you choose your specific role, before you queue up for a game.

  • Arcade

    • Arcade consists of different games that have different styles. This includes 1V1: Mystery Duel, 1v1 Limited Duel, 3v3 Elimination, 3v3 Lockout Elimination, 6v6 Lockout Elimination, 6v6 Mystery Heroes, 6v6 No Limits, Total Mayhem, Low Gravity, Capture the Flag, Deathmatch, Team Deathmatch, etc.

  • Competitive

    • Competitive is a gamemode where communication is necessary. There are 2 forms of Competitive Queue; Role Queue and Open Queue.

Heroes

Overwatch was release with 21 heroes. Ever since, a total of 11 heroes had been released. Heroes can be classified into three classes; Tank, Damage, and Support. Tanks are the frontline fighters of your team. Their duty is to lead the fight to capture objectives. Damage characters (also known as DPS) are the assassins, snipers, and soldiers who display destructive power. Their duty is to thoroughly disrupt the enemy team.
